The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1648, with some parts of the complex finished in subsequent years.

The Taj Mahal is widely recognized as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It combines elements of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles and is renowned for its stunning symmetry, intricate carvings, and the use of precious stones.

Visitors from around the globe come to admire its beauty, especially at sunrise and sunset when the marble appears to change color. The Taj Mahal is not only a symbol of love but also a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.
